Your machines, you already own. A laptop. A phone. A box in the closet, humming. Scattered, they sit, and the trust between them, you just... assume. That. That is the mistake.

Bard, a different way. A box, you make. A private room of your own. Nothing trusted because it sits on your network. "Inside the wall" means nothing here.

Watch. Four devices: a Mac, an iPhone, a Linux box, an Android. Each one, its own key it makes. Never shared. Never sent. To join, prove itself it must. And join, they do. All four.

Now. One of them speaks. A ping, across the box. The others, they hear it. Different machines, different networks. No shared password. No server in the middle you do not own.

Nothing leaves your network. Trust, earned, never given. Bard.
